Hot Air Balloon + Pyramids

Anthropology Museum
The world's finest pre-Columbian collection. The Aztec Sun Stone. The Maya room. World-class and a short hop through Chapultepec Park from Condesa.
Tue–Sun · 3–4 hrs · ~$5/pp

Chapultepec Castle
The only royal castle in North America. Steep walk up, panoramic city views at the top, gorgeous rooms inside. Worth every step.
Tue–Sun · 2–3 hrs · ~$5/pp
🚌
Turibus Hop-On Hop-Off
Four routes, one-day pass, audio guides in 9 languages. Relax on the open top deck and hop off wherever looks good. Great way to get oriented.
Daily 9am–7pm · all day · ~$20/pp
🛶
Xochimilco Canals
Colorful boats on ancient Aztec waterways — floating mariachis, vendors rowing up with food and drinks, pure festive joy. Pairs perfectly with Coyoacán after.
Daily 8am–5pm · half–full day
🍽️
High-End Group Dinner
One real splurge — private dining room for the group, top-tier contemporary Mexican cuisine. Sunday or Monday depending on the itinerary.
One evening · ~$150–200/pp

Good to know.

Includes champagne toast, buffet breakfast + optional 2hrs at the pyramids
Expect a slow, happy, tired afternoon after
🏛️ Museums
Both closed Mondays — all three options plan around this
Anthropology Museum: open Tue–Sun, 9am–5pm — budget at least 3 hrs
Chapultepec Castle: open Tue–Sun, 9am–5pm — steep uphill walk, wear good shoes
Both are in Chapultepec Park — doable in one comfortable day

Ancient Aztec canal system, UNESCO World Heritage site

While you're there

CDMX greatest hits
not in the plan, but worth knowing about.

🎨
Frida Kahlo Museum
La Casa Azul in Coyoacán — her actual home, full of her work and personal effects. One of the most visited museums in the country. Book ahead.
Book tickets in advance · Coyoacán
🏙️
Zócalo & Centro Histórico
The vast main plaza, Palacio de Bellas Artes, the Metropolitan Cathedral, the Templo Mayor ruins. The historic heart of the city and honestly overwhelming in the best way.
Free to walk · Full half-day
🛍️
Polanco
Mexico City's most upscale neighborhood — high-end restaurants, galleries, the Soumaya Museum (free, wild architecture), and great people-watching.
Turibus stops here · Walkable area
🌮
Mercado de San Juan
A beloved indoor market in Centro with gourmet vendors, imported goods, and some of the best tortas and quesadillas in the city. Low-key and real.
Open daily · Centro Histórico
🍺
Roma Norte
Our next-door neighborhood — walkable from Condesa. Licorería Limantour (one of the best bars in Latin America), excellent restaurants, and a great evening vibe.
Walk from Condesa · No reservations at bars
🌇
Mirador Torre Latinoamericana
The observation deck at the top of one of the city's most iconic skyscrapers. Sweeping 360° views of the entire valley. Best at dusk when the lights come on.
Centro · Evening recommended
